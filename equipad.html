EquiPad — Period Product Dispenser UX Study

Usability research and design evaluation for EquiPad’s prototype to improve accessibility and efficiency in public spaces.

Project Overview

EquiPad is a prototype for a public period product dispenser. Our research focused on understanding user interactions, accessibility challenges, and design improvements to enhance usability for diverse users.

  • Conducted usability testing with a diverse group of participants.
  • Evaluated user flows and interaction efficiency of the dispenser prototype.
  • Identified pain points and recommended design changes to improve accessibility.
  • Synthesized findings into actionable insights to guide product development.

Research Methods

  • Usability Testing: Observed participants interacting with the dispenser to identify friction points.
  • Interviews: Gathered in-depth feedback on experience, ease of use, and accessibility considerations.
  • Surveys: Collected quantitative data on preferences, satisfaction, and potential improvements.
  • Iterative Design Feedback: Incorporated insights into prototype refinements.

Tools & Key Details

  • Tools: Figma, Google Slides, Canva, Qualtrics
  • Methods: Usability Testing, Interviews, Surveys, Prototype Iteration
  • Results: Insights informed dispenser design updates; improved accessibility, reduced user friction, and streamlined interactions.
